Founder & head coach

Karim Ibrahim

Born in Cairo and developed at the famed Wadi Degla Club, Karim came up through the Egyptian junior system — ranked as high as No. 2 in his age group in the deepest junior squash nation on earth — and won the U.S. Junior Open U17 title in 2013 before competing on the PSA World Tour.

As a coach, he has trained elite juniors at Wadi Degla alongside mentor Karim Darwish, and served as a training partner to world champions Raneem El Welily and Nouran Gohar. In the U.S., he led programming as Head Squash Professional at the Arlen Specter US Squash Center in Philadelphia before being appointed Director of Junior National Teams for Team USA in 2023 — guiding the U.S. to its best-ever World Junior Championships finishes in 2024.

  • U.S. Junior Open U17 champion, 2013
  • Egyptian junior national team · ranked up to No. 2 in Egypt (junior)
  • Captain, St. Lawrence University men’s squash · PSA World Tour professional
  • Director of Junior National Teams, US Squash (2023– )
  • US Squash Level III certified coach
